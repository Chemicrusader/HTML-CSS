The Siamese Fighting Fish

The Fish in the Jar
A Siamese fighting fish of the Betta genus.

    "A fish with personality, a fish with class." -John Doe

  • This beautiful fish is sometimes called a Chinese or Japanese Fighting Fish. This is a misnomer because this fish is actually endemic to the Mekong basin.
  • Selective breeding has created a beautiful variety of colors and styles in this species.
  • The males of this species are highly aggressive toward each other. Often fighting to the death, they should only be kept alone or with different species.
  • In the past, Betta fighting was a sport in Thailand. With bets and rules similar to cock-fighting.
  • Sadly the natural version of this species is now listed as vulnerable by the IUCN due to habitat loss.
  • Unfortunately the majority of people that buy them seem to think they enjoy tiny living conditions. This may be due to the cups/jars they're kept in at most vendors. Bettas are actually happiest with at least 5 gallon tanks. For optimal conditions live plants are best and they do well with other non-aggresive species of similar size.
